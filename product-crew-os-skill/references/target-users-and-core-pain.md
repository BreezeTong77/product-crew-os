# Target Users and Core Pain

## Primary User 1: The Solo PM In A Small Team

### Profile

- Product manager in a small or midsize internet team.
- Often owns discovery, PRD, review, delivery follow-up, and stakeholder communication.
- May not have enough senior product support.

### Pain

- Does not know which product step should come next.
- Needs cross-functional challenges before formal review.
- Has to hold decisions, review items, and project context alone.
- Often writes documents without enough business, tech, data, or design feedback.

### Product Value

Product Crew OS gives this user:

- a visible coach
- a review room
- stage guidance
- reusable artifacts
- decision memory
- next-action pressure

## Primary User 2: The Growing Product Person

### Profile

- Junior or mid-level PM.
- Wants to improve product judgment, PRD quality, review readiness, and stakeholder communication.
- May be preparing for promotion, interview, or a larger product scope.

### Pain

- Can write features, but struggles with problem framing and product judgment.
- Does not know how business, engineering, data, design, QA, legal, and operations will challenge the work.
- Lacks realistic feedback before review meetings.

### Product Value

Product Crew OS turns product work into product training:

- every artifact receives structured pressure
- every role teaches a lens
- every review becomes a decision log
- every project builds PM judgment

## Primary User 3: The Founder PM

### Profile

- Founder, indie builder, or early-stage operator.
- Has product ideas but lacks a full product team.
- Needs to move from idea to validation, MVP, and launch without overbuilding.

### Pain

- Jumps into building too early.
- Lacks commercial, user, technical, and delivery challenge.
- Has no product office to keep the work honest.

### Product Value

Product Crew OS simulates a compact product office:

- business case first
- validation before PRD
- MVP boundary control
- staged stakeholder review
- artifact workspace for progress

## Secondary User: Product Lead / Team Lead

### Profile

- Product manager leading a small PM team.
- Wants consistent product process and review quality.

### Pain

- Team documents vary in quality.
- The same review issues appear repeatedly.
- Product process is tribal knowledge.

### Product Value

Product Crew OS creates:

- reusable workflow
- shared review standards
- artifact templates
- role-based review behavior
- training-by-doing for PMs

## Core Pain Statement

Product work is long, ambiguous, cross-functional, and emotionally lonely.

Most AI tools help the user produce text. Product Crew OS helps the user organize product work.

Core pain:

```text
I do not just need an answer. I need someone to know what stage I am in, challenge the weak parts, help me produce the right artifact, remember decisions, and tell me the next move.
```

## Jobs To Be Done

### Functional Job

When I am doing product work, I want to know the right next step and produce the right artifact, so that I can move the project forward with less rework.

### Emotional Job

When I am facing unclear product work, I want to feel less alone and less exposed, so that I can make decisions with more confidence.

### Social Job

When I bring work to stakeholders, I want my thinking to look structured and review-ready, so that others trust my product judgment.

## Main Switching Trigger

Users are most likely to use Product Crew OS when:

- they have a new product idea
- they need to write or review a PRD
- they feel blocked before a stakeholder meeting
- they need a product decision memo
- a project has too many scattered decisions
- they want to simulate review before real review

## Why Existing AI Chat Is Not Enough

Generic AI chat can answer, draft, and summarize.

It usually cannot reliably:

- keep a project state
- identify workflow stage
- call the right role only at the right time
- maintain review items and decision logs
- separate project memory from global rules
- feel like a consistent product office

This is the product wedge.
